The Score

The Goal

"What are we here to perform?"

In music, the score is the sheet music every musician follows. In a multi-agent AI system, the goal or task is the score. It tells the whole system what to accomplish — and keeps every agent aligned.

  • Could be a simple question or a complex multi-step project
  • Might take seconds — or many minutes — to accomplish
  • The clearer the goal, the better the performance

Example Goal

"Research our top 3 competitors, compare their pricing, and send me a summary email by end of day."

Orchestrator

The Conductor

"The intelligence that holds it all together"

The Conductor is the Orchestrator Agent — the strategic brain. It reads the goal, breaks it into steps, assigns those steps to the right agents, and watches over everything to ensure success.

  • Reads the goal and creates a plan of action
  • Decides which agents are needed and when
  • Adapts if something goes wrong mid-task
  • Assembles all results into a coherent final output

Analogy

Like a brilliant project manager who sees the whole picture, delegates smartly, and delivers.

Research Agents

The Strings

"The foundation — finding everything we need"

Like the strings that carry the melody, Research Agents do the foundational work. They search the web, read documents, query databases, and pull in all the raw material the Conductor needs.

  • Search the web for current information
  • Read uploaded documents and files
  • Query databases and external APIs
  • Return structured raw data back to the Conductor

In Action

Three Research Agents search competitor pricing pages simultaneously — done in minutes, not hours.

Analysis Agents

The Woodwinds

"Turning raw data into real insight"

Woodwinds add texture and depth. Analysis Agents do the same — they take raw information from Research Agents and extract meaning. They compare, synthesize, and prepare the data for action.

  • Summarize large amounts of text or data
  • Compare options and identify key differences
  • Score or rank findings against your criteria
  • Flag the most important discovery for the Conductor

In Action

Given pricing from three CRMs, spots that one is 40% cheaper for small teams — and highlights it.

Action Agents

The Brass

"Bold, direct — making things happen in the world"

Brass instruments declare and move you to action. Action Agents are where AI meets the real world — they draft documents, send emails, update records, and make things happen.

  • Draft polished emails, reports, and documents
  • Send communications and notifications
  • Update CRM records or project management tools
  • Trigger workflows in other software systems

In Action

Takes the analysis output, writes a crisp competitive summary with a comparison table, and sends it — no copy-paste required.

Memory Agents

The Percussion

"Keeping the beat — nothing falls through the cracks"

Percussion keeps the orchestra synchronized. Memory & Coordination Agents track what's been done, what's in progress, and what comes next. In complex workflows, they prevent missteps.

  • Remember context from earlier in the task
  • Track which steps are complete vs. still pending
  • Ensure agents don't repeat or contradict each other
  • Manage dependencies — "don't send until analysis is done"

In Action

Three parallel Research Agents finish at different times — Percussion tracks them all and signals the Conductor the moment every result is in.

Why It Matters

The Power of the Orchestra

This is why multi-agent AI is a fundamentally different kind of tool.

⚡

Parallel Performance

Multiple agents work simultaneously — like strings, woodwinds, and brass all playing at once. Tasks that would take hours alone can finish in minutes.

🎯

Specialized Expertise

Each agent is optimized for its specific role — just as a violinist doesn't play the drums. Specialization means higher quality at every step.

🧩

Complexity Made Simple

The Conductor handles the coordination so you don't have to. You give the goal — the orchestra figures out how to achieve it and reports back.
